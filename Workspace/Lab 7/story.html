Anthropocene

Steel droplets descend the fibers of a leaf,
True air has left us.
Natural become unnatural, and unnatural
accepted as norm.
Our devices have lead us to the forefront
of human capability.
At what cost, do we examine this fervent
effort to complexify our lives?
Air tangible, but not a product of tension.
For we poison our skies and headspace with
byproducts of tempory bliss concentrated in
disposable forms.
How to break the cycle; how to restore the world.
Our waters, acidified. Our air made polluted.
Our values, perverted. Our lives made polluted.
And yet, we advance.
The drive to conquer; man must own all, be masters of all, exploit all.
Our anthropocene brethren will be the end of us.
Steel droplets descend the folds of our skin.
True humans have left us.
Their values permeate yet; for all hope is not lost.
It is up to us, the inheritors of their destiny, to
fulfill our purpose.
To embody their pursuit of ideals, and all that is
good.
